## About the service

Gmail is Google's free webmail service. It is a legitimate, long-lived provider — not a disposable service — used by over 1.8 billion people worldwide for personal and business email.

## Typical use

General personal email. Many small businesses also operate on Gmail addresses before adopting custom domains.

## Why this page exists

People search "is gmail disposable" or "should I block gmail signups". This page exists to be unambiguous: **no**.

## Why you should NOT block @gmail.com signups

Gmail is one of the largest sources of legitimate user signups. The right move is to score Gmail signups normally while watching for the specific abuse patterns it enables:

- **Dot tricks** — `john.doe@gmail.com` and `johndoe@gmail.com` deliver to the same inbox.
- **`+` suffix tricks** — `johndoe+spam1@gmail.com` and `johndoe+spam2@gmail.com` are the same person.

Vouchley's `/v1/verify` endpoint normalises these aliases when scoring. You get a `normalized_email` hint in the response so you can dedupe at signup.
